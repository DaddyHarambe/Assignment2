What are the Job Titles for your group's ideal jobs? How do each of these rank in terms of demand from employers?

Within our group we have three different career paths each of us wish to pursue in the near future. These include business analyst; development operations engineer and Information technology analyst. On job outlook job growth and demand are rated from the follow ascending order decline, stable, moderate growth, strong growth and very strong growth. On this spectrum business analyst are expected to have very strong growth within the next 5 years highlighting the high demand for future business analyst. On the contrary development operations engineers according to seek.com is projected to grow by 30% within the next 5 years. Furthermore, cyber security analyst is also expected to grow by 19.8% in the next 5 years. To keep everything constant IT business analyst jobs are also expected to grow by 27.7% within the next 5 years.

From your group's ideal jobs, you can identify a set of skills required for these jobs (we will refer to this as your group's required skill set). These can be divided into general skills (communication, problem solving, writing etc) and IT-specific skills (Javascript, SQL, etc).

At the base level all these jobs will require a degree in a related field such as a bachelor's degree in information technology or a degree in computer science. However, this will only assist one in the employment process and does not guarantee a job as another person without a degree may still obtain the same role. It is critical business analyst have well developed communication skills, team building skills, planning, leadership and time management as their focus will be largely dependent on leading a team and communicating data back to the business. Whereas devops engineer must be proficient with multiple coding languages such as Java, C++, python etc. Most also obtain a computer science degree. They must also familiarise themselves with cloud technologies. Many of those who wish to pursue this career should also consider doing a master's degree. Cybersecurity analyst on the other hand must be comfortable with trouble shooting, problem solving and have a keen eye for attention to detail. A cybersecurity analyst ensures the protection of a company's networks so it is critical when an issue occurs, they do not panic and fix the problem with efficiency. Cybersecurity analysts are recommended to be at least proficient in two programming languages and typically have a degree in cybersecurity.
